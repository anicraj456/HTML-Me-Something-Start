Homemade Biryani Masala

Perfect Masala to make Aromatic Biryani

Biryani is one of the most amazing royal delicacies introduced to Indians by the Persians.
Since then it has been much popular and is considered to be a luxurious treat to enjoy on special occasions.

Ingredients :

  • 1" 5-6nos Cinnamon sticks
  • 15 nos Green Cardamom
  • 8 nos Cloves
  • Small piece Nutmeg
  • 2 nos Mice
  • 2 nos Star Anise
  • 4 nos Bay Leaves
  • 4 nos Black Cardamom
  • 1 Tsp Shah Jeera
  • 1 TBsp Fennel Seeds
  • 1 TBsp Cumin Seeds
  • 2 TBsp Coriander Seeds
  • 1 Tsp peppercorns
  • 1 TBsp Pomegranate Seeds
  • 1/4 Tsp Tumeric Powder
  • 1 Tsp Red Chilli Powder
Directions :
  1. Take all the whole spices in a wide pan. Roughly crush the nutmeg in mortar and pestle before adding it into the pan.
    Dry roast them on medium-low heat until they are aromatic. Once roasted remove it to a plate and let them cool down completely.
  2. Transfer to the blender or spice grinder.
  3. Make it to a fine powder. Then add turmeric powder and red chili powder.
  4. Again grind for a minite,so spice powders are mixed well.
Storage & Self Life :
  1. This masala powder must be stored in an airtight container or jar.
  2. Keep the jar in a cool dry space, away from sunlight.
  3. It stays fresh for 3-4 months or longer depends on dry/cold weather.
  4. Always use the clean and dry spoon.
How To Use In The Recipe :
  1. Add 1 teaspoon of biryani masala for 1 cup of basmati rice. → Check here to see how to make Briyani..
  2. This can be used in making curry, sabzi in place of garam masala for a different flavor.
